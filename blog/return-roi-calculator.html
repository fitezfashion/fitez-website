Why Returns Are Costly for Online Fashion Brands

Ecommerce fashion returns are a complex and expensive challenge. Unlike electronics or books, clothing presents subjective fit issues that create uncertainty for shoppers. Return rates for clothing typically land in the 24â€“26% range in both the US and UK, with subcategories like fast fashion, womenswear, and shoes regularly exceeding 30%. US market research confirms that apparel accounts for more returns than any other product type.

The vast majority of these returns are due to fit and size issues: up to 67% of fashion returns are attributed to sizing, with "too small" being the most frequent complaint. "Bracketing," the practice of ordering multiple sizes and returning unwanted ones, continues to rise. Inaccurate size charts, inconsistent fit across brands, and the inability to try before buying drive these figures higher.

Returns aren't just a logistics issue, they translate to real financial loss. US retailers face nearly $890 billion in returns annually, accounting for over 17% of all retail sales. The direct cost per return (postage, processing, repackaging) typically ranges from $10â€“$25 per item in the US and UK. Hidden costs include restocking, damaged inventory, and lost sales opportunities.
