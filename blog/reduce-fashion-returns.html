All Factors Causing Returns of Clothes in Fashion Ecommerce

Clothing returns due to size issues are a persistent and costly challenge in fashion ecommerce. With return rates for online apparel purchases soaring well above those in brick-and-mortar stores, understanding the full spectrum of factors driving these returns is vital for brands aiming to reduce costs and improve customer satisfaction.

The Scale of the Problem

Fashion ecommerce faces some of the highest return rates in retail. Recent studies show that the average return rate for online apparel orders is between 20.8% and 24.4% in the US, with some brands experiencing rates as high as 40%. Globally, fit and sizing issues account for over half of these returns—53% according to Prime AI and 70% according to McKinsey & Company. In fact, 77% of returns in the fashion sector are due to incorrect sizing, making it the number one reason for returns in this industry.

A staggering 88% of consumers have returned clothing orders at some point, and over 56% of total ecommerce returns come from clothing items alone. The financial impact is immense: US apparel returns are estimated to cost $38 billion annually, with $25 billion attributed to processing costs alone.

Key Factors Causing Clothing Returns in Fashion Ecommerce

1. Sizing and Fit Issues

  • Inconsistent Sizing Across Brands: Lack of standardization means a "medium" in one brand may fit like a "large" in another, confusing shoppers and leading to high return rates.
  • Inadequate Size Guides: Poor or generic size charts, or missing information about how clothes fit different body types, leave customers guessing.
  • Bracketing Behavior: Many shoppers order multiple sizes of the same item, intending to return those that don't fit. This is especially common where free returns are offered.
  • Body Diversity: Standard size charts often fail to accommodate the wide range of body shapes, leading to dissatisfaction even when the "right" size is chosen.

2. Product Misrepresentation

  • Color and Material Discrepancies: Items may look different in real life due to lighting or editing in product photos, or may be made from unexpected materials.
  • Style Mismatches: The cut or drape of clothing may not match the appearance in photos, leading to unmet expectations.
  • Overstated Quality: Enhanced imagery or misleading descriptions can inflate expectations, resulting in disappointment and returns.

3. Faults and Defects

  • Order Fulfillment Errors: 52% of consumers return items because they are damaged or defective upon arrival.
  • Manufacturing Imperfections: Loose threads, poor stitching, or other quality issues prompt returns, especially when shoppers cannot inspect items before purchase.

4. Wrong Item Sent

  • Damaged or Defective Items: Mistakes in picking, packing, or shipping can result in customers receiving the wrong size, color, or even a completely different product.

5. Changing Customer Preferences

  • Impulse Buying and Regret: The ease of online shopping sometimes leads to impulse purchases, which are later returned when customers reconsider.
  • Seasonal or Occasion-Based Purchases: Items bought for specific events are often returned if they arrive too late or are no longer needed.

6. Liberal Return Policies

  • Encouraging Returns: Generous return policies, while boosting conversion rates, can inadvertently encourage bracketing and higher return rates.

Severity and Brand Impact

  • Brands Affected: Nearly all fashion brands selling online face this issue. According to Coresight, 58% of retailers report at least 20% of online clothing orders are returned, and 45% have seen return rates rise in recent years.
  • Severity by Category: Pants are the most frequently returned item (65% of apparel returns), while underwear and lingerie are the least.
  • Customer Loyalty: 32% of customers would stop shopping with a brand after a single poor experience, such as receiving ill-fitting clothing.

Why Do Size Issues Cause So Many Returns?

1. Lack of Standardized Sizing

One of the core reasons for clothing returns due to size issues is the absence of universal sizing standards. Each brand interprets sizing differently, so a "medium" in one label may fit like a "large" in another, leaving shoppers confused and increasing the likelihood of returns.

2. Inaccurate or Incomplete Size Guides

Many retailers provide generic or overly complex size charts, forcing customers to rely on guesswork or self-measurement. This leads to frequent mismatches between customer expectations and actual fit, especially for international shoppers unfamiliar with regional sizing conventions.

3. Bracketing Behavior

To compensate for sizing uncertainty, shoppers often engage in "bracketing"—ordering multiple sizes of the same item and returning those that don’t fit. Research shows that nearly 15% of returns from multi-brand retailers are due to bracketing, further inflating return rates.

4. Diverse Body Types

The diversity of body shapes and proportions means that standardized size charts rarely accommodate everyone. For men’s apparel, 23% of returns are due to items fitting too small, while in womenswear, 22% of returns are because garments are too large. Children’s clothing sees similar trends, with 31% of returns due to items being too small.

The Financial and Operational Impact

The consequences of clothing returns due to size issues are severe. Returns erode profit margins through restocking, shipping, and processing costs. Less than half of returned clothing items are resold at full price, and only 20% are actually defective, meaning most returns are purely due to fit or sizing dissatisfaction. For brands, this translates into billions in lost revenue and increased operational complexity.

How Many Brands Are Affected?
